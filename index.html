GUIDE TO THAILAND ON A BUDGET

  • Destinations
  • Costs
  • Budget Tips
  • Activities

Thailand is the travel hub of Southeast Asia. I spent three months in the country back in 2013 and have been itching to go back since! It’s an easy country to travel and you can meet a lot of other people there. Thailand is a well-worn destination on the backpacking trail and everything is convenient and easy. However, there are still many off the beaten path destinations away from the crowds and the prices they bring. Overall, Thailand speaks for itself. When you hear its name, you already think about beaches, beauty, jungles, and food. And your thoughts are on point.

Everyday Costs

Accommodation – Thailand is very cheap, though the north is far cheaper than Bangkok and the southern islands. You can find cheap guesthouses for as little as 300 Baht/night in cities and 200 Baht/night in the countryside. To get the prices in US dollars then divide the baht by 35. You can get around for an average of $10/night.

Food – Food is really cheap in Thailand. Street food costs as little as 20 baht, though on average you’ll spend about 35-50 THB per meal if you want something filling. If you stick to the local street food, you can eat for around 120-170 baht/day. Since food is so cheap, there’s no point in grocery shopping.

Budgeting Tips

Go local – The easiest way to save money in Thailand is to simply live like a local. Take local buses, eat street food, and drink local beer.

Stay in hostels – Hostels are economical as well as the norm for Thailand.

Couchsurf— And if you're too broke for a hostel, nothing’s cheaper than sleeping for free!
